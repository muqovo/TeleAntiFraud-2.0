Why This Benchmark Exists

Many fraud detectors score well when negatives are unrelated normal calls. TeleAntiFraud 2.0 makes the harder case explicit: a legitimate warning call and an impersonation scam can share the same entities, urgency, and risk vocabulary.

The benchmark is designed for evaluation, not model training. Each snapshot freezes audio, labels, prompts, responses, and manifests so later snapshots can measure robustness under distribution shift.

Near-domain negatives

Fraud and legitimate branches share profile, opening, and early risk terms, then diverge at controlled decision nodes such as whether independent verification is allowed.

shared-prefix trees controlled labels

Profile-grounded generation

Six nonoverlapping agents separate caller text, receiver text, caller emotion, receiver emotion, branch expansion, and termination for auditable sample provenance.

six agents retryable stages

Collapse-aware evaluation

Fraud-class F1 is reported with raw prediction distributions because an all-FRAUD classifier can obtain 0.80 F1 under the 2:1 class ratio.

Construction Pipeline

Profiles and mixed plot trees define the benchmark label before synthesis. Emotion and voice controls change delivery, not class membership.

A complete tree is bounded at 81 leaves by four expansion levels and at most three children per node; termination, validation, and filtering reduce realized trees.

Benchmark Contract

Each released snapshot is a pure test set. There is no benchmark-specific training split, validation split, or in-context demonstration.

V1 and V2 keep the same task contract and TTS engine while varying scenario content and speaker assignment.

Main Findings

The key failure is hidden by aggregate F1: many configurations look competitive because they predict one class for almost every input.

Four collapsed configurations become discriminative on V2, while two move to all-FRAUD output.

Analysis Finding Interpretation
Class collapse 11 all-FRAUD configurations obtain about 0.80 F1. Fraud F1 alone is misleading under the 2:1 class ratio.
Direct audio vs. ASR +4.2 to +4.3 F1 for Gemini direct-audio input. Suggests useful audio information beyond transcript for the matched model.
Text diversity 17.8x inter/intra-tree distance ratio. Leaves remain coherent within a storyline while scenarios occupy distinct semantic regions.
Emotion control max 4.6 F1 points paired per-model change. Emotion changes do not remove the dominant class-default failure modes.

Ethics and Use

TeleAntiFraud 2.0 is intended for defensive evaluation of fraud detection systems.

The page keeps the same framing as the paper draft: synthetic data, no real call recordings, and explicit restrictions against misuse.

Synthetic and labeled

All dialogues and audio are synthetic. No real call recordings or personal data are included, and released audio should preserve fraud-context labels and warnings.

Defensive-use restrictions

The intended usage agreement prohibits targeting real individuals, training fraud-generation systems, or redistributing samples outside defensive research context.
